Let's Learn the Fundamental Jargons




Rental Yield

Rental Yield is the net money made by the property through rent with respect to the purchase price of the property. This is similar to dividends in the stock market, where the rent acts as the dividend. Moreover, houses having a rental yield of greater than 5% are considered to be good investments.



Capital Gain

Capital Gain is the increment in the value of the property from the time of purchase. This can be related to how the value of a stock can increase in value after the time of purchase. If the value of the underlying neighborhood lowers on a yearly basis, you are losing on your investment money. Hence, investing in rising localities is important. Aiming for postcodes with a 10% rise in capital gains over the last 5 years is a great starting point.

Liquidity

Liquidity is how quickly you sell or buy property. Even with great property yield and capital gains, if you are unable to quickly sell the property when prices are high, you lose out on potential profit. Hence, making sure that the locality is one with high activity and demand is crucial.
